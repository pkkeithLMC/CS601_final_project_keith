Philosophy of Education

Understanding your students is paramount to productive education.

I believe that to educate students, a teacher must consider the students’ past performance and learning profile. A large majority of students have not had their learning styles, learning disabilities and learning profile taking into consideration. To understand how to help students make progress, I must understand the primary barrier to their acquisition of new skills. I document the students learning profile by identifying the students’ neurodevelopmental strengths and weaknesses and find ways to leverage the student’s strengths to overcome areas of weakness. I use that information to demystify the learning process with the students, differentiate their instruction and teach them how to modify a lesson that does not meet their individual needs into a lesson that engages their strengths.

Mathematics/CS education requires a solid foundation.

Mathematics/CS is inherently very abstract, and as a mathematician and CS professional, I am very comfortable with this level of abstraction. However, many students are not and require a grounding of the concepts in the real world. I believe that the introduction of new material should begin with concrete examples, move to representative problems and finally implement the abstract. I also promote good problem solving strategies, reasonableness testing and answer checking techniques. I don’t consider a problem complete unless a student has determined if their answer is reasonable and have checked their answer.

Learning Mathematics/CS is a process of making connections.

A good mathematician and computer scientist has a toolkit full of procedures and techniques that they use to solve problems. A part of learning to solve new problems involves showing students how to use the tools that they already know in a different order or in a different way. When teaching a new objective, I always highlight these connections or compare/contrast this objective to a previous skill. I improve their problem solving abilities by working on pattern recognition so that when they encounter a new problem, they can apply the skills in their toolkit because they recognize that they have seen something similar before.
